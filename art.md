timeline
    title Art History Timeline

    section Ancient Art
        30,000 BCE : Prehistoric Art
                   : Cave paintings, Venus figurines
        3000 BCE   : Ancient Egyptian Art
                   : Pyramids, hieroglyphics, sculptures
        800 BCE    : Ancient Greek Art
                   : Classical sculpture, pottery, architecture
        200 BCE    : Ancient Roman Art
                   : Realistic portraits, frescoes, mosaics

    section Medieval Period
        400 CE     : Byzantine Art
                   : Religious icons, mosaics, illuminated manuscripts
        800 CE     : Islamic Art
                   : Geometric patterns, calligraphy, architecture
        1000 CE    : Romanesque Art
                   : Churches, stone sculptures, manuscripts
        1200 CE    : Gothic Art
                   : Cathedrals, stained glass, pointed arches

    section Renaissance
        1400       : Early Renaissance
                   : Perspective, humanism, classical revival
        1500       : High Renaissance
                   : Masterpiece period, perfect harmony
        1520       : Mannerism
                   : Elongated forms, complex compositions

    section Baroque & Rococo
        1600       : Baroque
                   : Drama, movement, light and shadow
        1720       : Rococo
                   : Ornate, pastel colors, asymmetrical

    section Modern Movements
        1800       : Neoclassicism
                   : Return to classical ideals
        1850       : Romanticism
                   : Emotion, nature, individualism
        1860       : Realism
                   : Everyday subjects, social issues
        1870       : Impressionism
                   : Light, color, outdoor painting
        1880       : Post-Impressionism
                   : Personal expression, symbolic content
        1905       : Fauvism
                   : Bold colors, wild brushstrokes
        1907       : Cubism
                   : Geometric forms, multiple perspectives
        1910       : Abstract Expressionism
                   : Non-representational, emotional
        1916       : Dada
                   : Anti-art, absurd, provocative
        1920       : Surrealism
                   : Dreams, unconscious mind

    section Contemporary
        1950       : Pop Art
                   : Popular culture, mass media
        1960       : Minimalism
                   : Simple forms, industrial materials
        1970       : Conceptual Art
                   : Ideas over aesthetics
        1980       : Postmodernism
                   : Pluralism, questioning narratives
        2000       : Digital Art
                   : New media, technology-based
